Synopsis

• This course will cover the theory of structural dynamics. Students will learn to mathematically describe the response of SDOF systems with and without damping for free and harmonic vibration due to arbitrary excitations. Students will learn to compute the dynamic response of structural components and structural systems under dynamic loads. The syllabus will cover lumped and distributed mass systems including modal analysis of MDOF systems
• The second part of the course covers the basic of aeroelasticity.
Students will learn the basic concept of aeroelasticity and
phenomena which occur on aircraft structures such as divergence
and flutter. Students will get a chance to learn about unsteady
aerodynamics, flutter analysis and flutter suppression.

• 3rd - Fatigue due to vibration

Motivation

• As engineers, why do we need to learned

structural dynamics?

Vibrations in machines, vehicles and structures are typically undesirable as they produce stresses, energy losses and increased loads. They contribute to structural wear, reduced accuracy and can lead to passenger discomfort in vehicles, structural failure that can lead to destruction and loss of life.
